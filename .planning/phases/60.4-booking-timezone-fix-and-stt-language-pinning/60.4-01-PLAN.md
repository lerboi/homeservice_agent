<objective>
Stream A structural fix. Eliminate the intermittent +8h booking offset (caller asks for 3 PM SG, Google Calendar event lands at 11 PM) by:
1. Plumbing `tenant_timezone` through `book_appointment → calendar_push → google_calendar`.
2. Setting `start.timeZone` + `end.timeZone` on the Google Calendar `events.insert()` body, using naive local ISO for `dateTime` (per RESEARCH Pitfall 1 — passing both UTC offset + timeZone double-offsets Google's event).
3. Changing all 3 `America/Chicago` fallback sites to UTC + structured WARN.
4. Ensuring `_ensure_utc_iso` coerces-and-logs (never raises) per D-A-04.

Purpose: close the root-cause hole identified during discuss — when Gemini strips the UTC offset from slot_start, Google interprets the naive `dateTime` in the calendar's default TZ (not tenant's), yielding SG's UTC+8 shift. Adding `timeZone` to the event body makes Google interpret `dateTime` in the tenant TZ regardless of Gemini's behaviour.

Output: shipped code on livekit-agent/main (Railway auto-deploys); unit test suite proving structural correctness + fallback behavior; zero behavior change for US tenants whose calendar default matches their TZ.
